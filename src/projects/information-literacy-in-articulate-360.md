# Information Literacy in Articulate 360

<img src="{{ '/images/projects/information-literacy.png' | url }}" alt="Information Literacy course in Articulate" class="post-featured-image" />

<div class="problem-solution">
  <div class="problem-solution-col">
    <div class="section-eyebrow">The Problem</div>
    <p>Students struggle to evaluate online sources critically, especially in a digital environment where misinformation is widespread.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="problem-solution-col">
    <div class="section-eyebrow">The Solution</div>
    <p>A collaborative e-learning course built in Articulate Rise 360, grounded in Mike Caulfield's SIFT Method, later adapted for live use in a U.S. History course.</p>
  </div>
</div>

This information literacy course was developed alongside my classmates Emily Abernethy, Paul Jensen, and Penny Pells. It relies on the [SIFT Method developed by Mike Caulfield](https://hapgood.us/2019/06/19/sift-the-four-moves/) and is something I ended up tweaking for actual use inside of a U.S. History course.

[Take the Information Literacy course in Articulate Rise](https://rise.articulate.com/share/U7sa7t6mWCySMajS9rwDuuC2wr6567F5#/)
