# Teaching About OER

<img src="{{ '/images/projects/teaching-oer.png' | url }}" alt="Teaching About OER tutorial" class="post-featured-image" />

<div class="problem-solution">
  <div class="problem-solution-col">
    <div class="section-eyebrow">The Problem</div>
    <p>Subject matter experts joining Lumen needed a clear introduction to open education resources without sitting through lengthy in-person training.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="problem-solution-col">
    <div class="section-eyebrow">The Solution</div>
    <p>A self-paced H5P tutorial distilling a complex multi-part training into something SMEs could complete independently.</p>
  </div>
</div>

In the 6110 Instructional Design course, I worked on creating training materials for subject matter experts. One aspect of their introduction to Lumen Learning is learning about open education resources. I made this tutorial using H5P: [Understanding, Finding, and Using OER](https://lumenlearning.h5p.com/content/1291166031690563998).

Looking back on this project several years later, it's remarkable to note how far things have shifted in just a few short years. Now that AI can create OER in seconds, the work of carefully finding and reusing it is less important. I still appreciate this interactive as a resource in demonstrating how a complex and multi-part training with several pages of documentation (plus an hour-long in-person meeting), was distilled down into a brief tutorial.
