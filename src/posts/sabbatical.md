# Sabbatical

<img src="{{ '/images/Nyhavn.JPG' | url }}" alt="Friends and family at Nyhavn in Copenhagen" class="post-featured-image" />

April was a wonderful, full, slightly chaotic month — and I loved every bit of it.

Lumen gave me a month-long sabbatical as a 10-year anniversary gift. I used it a year late, which is honestly very on-brand for me, but it was a fantastic and much-appreciated break. The family and I spent about two weeks in Europe, bouncing between Denmark, Germany, and the Netherlands, visiting dear friends and family along the way. It was the kind of trip where you come home a little changed — not dramatically, not in a way you can fully articulate, but just... softer somehow. More grateful for things.

## Denmark

We started in Copenhagen with friends who have been living in Denmark for several years now and were generous enough to show us around. Copenhagen is everything you've heard it is: beautiful, walkable, expensive, and full of people who seem to have figured something out about how to live. We wandered through Nyhavn (pictured above — those colorful buildings are even more striking in person), ate a lot of good food, and soaked in as much of the city as we could.

<img src="{{ '/images/castle.JPG' | url }}" alt="Inside a Danish palace with friends, everyone in blue shoe covers" class="post-inline-image" />

We also managed to get into a palace — blue shoe covers and all — which felt very European and slightly absurd in the best possible way.

<img src="{{ '/images/castle.JPG' | url }}" alt="Inside a Danish palace with friends, everyone in blue shoe covers" class="post-inline-image" />

After Copenhagen, we headed to Aarhus, and made a detour to Billund, which happens to be the hometown of Lego. My daughter was delighted.

<img src="{{ '/images/legostore.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Jumping in front of a giant Lego sculpture in Billund, Denmark" class="post-inline-image" />

The Danes also have this pick-and-mix candy culture — huge walls of bulk candy that you fill a bag with by weight — that my children are now deeply committed to importing to North Carolina. (They will not succeed, but I appreciate the passion.)

<img src="{{ '/images/candywall.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Kids at a Candy King pick-and-mix candy wall in Denmark" class="post-inline-image" />

## Germany

From Denmark we traveled to Germany to visit another dear friend, with a quick stop in Bremen on the way. Bremen is charming in a quieter way than Copenhagen — medieval market square, Gothic cathedral, Beck's brewery around the corner — and it was a lovely few hours before we continued on.

<img src="{{ '/images/Bremen.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Family in front of Bremen Cathedral" class="post-inline-image" />

## The Netherlands

We arrived in Amsterdam just as the blossoms were coming in, which felt like an almost embarrassingly perfect piece of timing. My brother recently started a new job there, so we got to spend time with him and his family, which was a real treat.

<img src="{{ '/images/canals.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Family at an Amsterdam canal" class="post-inline-image" />

We spent a day out at Zaanse Schans to see the windmills and then drove out to the tulip fields, which was exactly as spectacular as it sounds.

<img src="{{ '/images/stroopwafels.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Group at Zaanse Schans windmills, eating stroopwafels" class="post-inline-image" />

We rented a Dutch cargo bike — a bakfiets, which is essentially a wooden box on wheels with a bicycle attached — and piled the kids in, which was chaotic and wonderful.

<img src="{{ '/images/tulipstop.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Group at the Dutch tulip fields with a cargo bike, bright pink and red flowers stretching to the horizon" class="post-inline-image" />

<img src="{{ '/images/bikingtulips.jpeg' | url }}" alt="Wendy at the tulip fields" class="post-inline-image" />

<img src="{{ '/images/posts/europe-wendy-tulips.jpg' | url }}" alt="Wendy sitting in a Dutch tulip field" class="post-inline-image" />

## Back home

We came home to a tired family and, in my case, a solid week of being sick. I had just enough energy to slowly work through a spring cleaning purge that I'd been putting off for months, which felt like a reasonable use of my compromised state. Once I was functional again, I turned my attention to a side project I'd been chipping away at during the quieter moments of the trip — an improv website, of all things.

More on that in the [next post](/posts/the-improv-hub/).
