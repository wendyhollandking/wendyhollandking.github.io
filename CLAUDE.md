# Wendy King — Site Context for Claude

## About This Site

This is Wendy King's personal website, built to establish a professional brand in ed tech and instructional design. It houses a portfolio and blog. The goal is to attract higher-paying roles in ed tech or similar fields. Blog posts may also be shared on LinkedIn.

## About Wendy

Wendy is an instructional designer and former classroom teacher with 15+ years of experience. She started as a high school social studies and psychology teacher (AP Psychology, AP World History, Geography, U.S. History), then moved into online learning design. Since 2014, she's been a Course Product Manager at Lumen Learning, where she architects OER-based digital courseware across the social sciences. In 2024, she returned to the classroom as an Adjunct Professor of Psychology at Davidson College, teaching a flipped, application-based Intro Psych course. She holds an MEd in Learning Design & Technology from UNC Charlotte and a BS in Social Sciences Teaching from BYU.

She thinks like a teacher even when she's not in the classroom. She is thorough, research-oriented, and deeply committed to equity in education — particularly access to high-quality learning for underserved students. She is a strong supporter of DEI work and believes education should be more just and equitable.

She is currently blogging about AI and sharing how it benefits her work in instructional design.

## Writing Voice

Wendy's writing is conversational, warm, and substantive — approachable but intelligent. She enjoys playing with words and sentences. Her style features:

- Longer sentences with natural rhythm; she uses commas and semi-colons freely, and ellipses occasionally
- Little quips, asides, and moments of humor — she likes to sound like a real person
- Stories and connection; she writes to engage, not to impress
- Claims backed by research and evidence — she is an academic at heart and won't state things without grounding
- A preference for directness (something she's actively working toward)

**Em-dashes:** Use selectively, not as a default connector.

**Avoid entirely:**
- AI-sounding jargon: leverage, robust, facilitate, streamline, benchmark, delve, seamless, transformative, cutting-edge, game-changing, unlock, empower, harness
- Short, punchy, declarative sentences stacked together (a hallmark of AI-generated writing)
- Predictable clichés and filler transitions
- Pompous or performative language that signals effort rather than demonstrating it

**Instead:** Varied sentence structure, a human voice, specific concrete details, and the kind of humor or warmth that makes a reader feel like they're talking to a smart friend.

## Collaboration Preferences

- Work as a partner: suggest ideas, flag things that seem off, offer alternatives
- Always support claims with evidence when writing content
- Don't rewrite Wendy's prose unless asked — preserve her voice
- If drafting from scratch, aim to match the voice described above
- Ask before making structural or design changes that go beyond what was requested
